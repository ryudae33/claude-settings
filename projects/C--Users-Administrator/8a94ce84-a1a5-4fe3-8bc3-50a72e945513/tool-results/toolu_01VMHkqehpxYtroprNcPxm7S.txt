  1346→  1346→8. Maintenance
  1347→  1347→WARNING:
  1348→  1348→An overall inspection of OnRobot's end-of-arm tooling and accessories must be
  1349→  1349→performed regularly and at least once every 6 months. This inspection must
  1350→  1350→include but is not limited to check for defective material and clean gripping
  1351→  1351→surfaces.
  1352→  1352→Use original spare parts, and original service instructions for OnRobot's end-of-
  1353→  1353→arm tooling, accessories and the robot. Failure to comply with this precaution
  1354→  1354→can cause unexpected risks, resulting in severe injury.
  1355→  1355→If you have questions regarding spare parts and repair, please visit our website
  1356→  1356→www.onrobot.com to contact us.
  1357→  1357→8.1. Screwdriver
  1358→  1358→Screw Fix and Bit
  1359→  1359→NOTE:
  1360→  1360→The screw fixes and bits are wear and tear parts, therefore they must be
  1361→  1361→inspected regularly.
  1362→  1362→These parts must be inspected minimum once a week or after 60.000 cycles.
  1363→  1363→If a screw fix is damaged due to misuse, it must be replaced.
  1364→  1364→If the Pick Screw process is failing, it might be an indicator that the screw fix is worn or has
  1365→  1365→been damaged.
  1366→  1366→WARNING:
  1367→  1367→The Screw-bit System should be visually inspected every time the robot is
  1368→  1368→reset or once per week, depending on application and use.
  1369→  1369→WARNING:
  1370→  1370→Check the inside of the screw bit holder regularly for debris. Make sure that the
  1371→  1371→magnetic interface is clear from metal shavings and dirt.
  1372→  1372→WARNING:
  1373→  1373→Always detach the Screwdriver from the Quick Changer to perform cleaning or
  1374→  1374→maintenance.

  1833→  1833→9. Troubleshooting
  1834→  1834→9.1. Robot Has Not Obtained an IP Address
  1835→  1835→If the Compute Box has not assigned an IP address to the robot, do the following:
  1836→  1836→Assign a static IP address to the robot that matches your current IP settings on your Compute
  1837→  1837→Box. The default IP address of the Compute Box is 192.168.1.1.
  1838→  1838→NOTE:
  1839→  1839→Change the last number in the IP address (if using 255.255.255.0 subnet
  1840→  1840→mask) to avoid an IP conflict with the Compute Box.
  1841→  1841→Example
  1842→  1842→If the default fixed (192.168.1.1) IP address is used on the Compute Box, then use the following
  1843→  1843→values:
  1844→  1844→•
  1845→  1845→IP address: 192.168.1.2
  1846→  1846→•
  1847→  1847→Subnet Mask: 255.255.255.0
  1848→  1848→9.2. Changing the DIP Switch Does Not Take Effect
  1849→  1849→To change the DIP switch network settings, first change the DIP switches and then cycle the
  1850→  1850→Compute Box/Eye Box power so the changes will take effect.
  1851→  1851→If the changes still do not take effect, wait one minute and then cycle the Compute Box/Eye
  1852→  1852→Box power once again.
