   127→Overview of this machine 
   128→Thank you very much for selecting our Automatic Screw Feeder "NSRI series". This machine, 
   129→with the screwing robot, can line up screws (M-1, M-3) and supplies them one by one to an 
   130→automatic machine to help streamline screw tightening.  
   131→Different sizes of screws can be used by changing the rail, escaper, passing plate and the robot 
   132→escaper guide. It can be used wherever there is a power source for an AC adapter. 
   133→

   137→• Operation Manual 1 copy

   141→• Ground Wire 1 piece

   468→5.2 Basic operation 
   469→5.2.1 Amount of screws to be loaded 
   470→1. Turn the power switch ON and OFF so that the scooping block is at the lower limit 
   471→position. 
   472→2. Load screws up to approximately 2 ～ 3mm below the rail surface. 
   473→3. At this time, check that screws are not loaded so as to cover the upper portion of the 
   474→inclined plate. 
   475→4. Be sure to determine the screw load by observing the machine while it is in operation. 
   476→ 
   477→CAUTION: 
   478→An excessive amount of screws, loaded into the chamber, will have an adverse 
   479→effect on the screw alignment and transport.

   496→ 
   497→5.2.2 Loading the screws 
   498→1. Open the upper cover. 
   499→2. When the chamber plates are at their lowest position, load screws up to 2 to 3 mm below 
   500→the rail groove surface. 
   501→3. Check that the screws are not loaded so as to cover the upper portion of the inclined 
   502→plates. 
   503→ 
   504→CAUTION: 
   505→Do not overload the chamber with screws otherwise it may cause a malfunction 
   506→or damage the machine. 
   507→ 
   508→5.2.3 Turning ON the power 
   509→1. Plug the attached AC adapter into the main body and power outlet. 
   510→2. When the power switch is turned ON, the power switch lamp lights up. The scooping block 
   511→starts to move up and down. The rail starts to vibrate, and the escaper starts rotating. 
   512→3. Screws move along the rail towards the rotating escaper which selects one screw at a 
   513→time. 
   514→4. The escaper rotates and deposits the selected screw at the pickup site. 
   515→5. At this moment, the sensor detects a screw and the screw sensor LED lights up and then 
   516→the operation stops. 
   517→6. When a screw is picked up, the sensor detects this, and the sensor LED light goes off and 
   518→the machine resumes operation. 
   519→ 
   520→CAUTION: 
   521→Do not use any AC adapter other than the one included with this unit, as it may 
   522→cause damage.

   605→5.5 Checking and adjusting the rail vibration 
   606→This machine’s rail vibration can be adjusted. 
   607→The screw transport speed differs depending on the screw type. Check the screw transport 
   608→speed. If the rail vibration hinders a smooth transport of the screws, it can be adjusted. 
   609→1. Loosen the anti-vibration screw at the rear of the machine. 
   610→2. Next, turn the vibration adjusting screw, located on the bottom of the machine, to 
   611→adjust the vibration. 
   612→When the screw is turned clockwise, as viewed from the bottom of the machine, the 
   613→vibration will increase. 
   614→When the screw is turned counterclockwise, the vibration decreases. 
   615→If the vibration is adjusted to a too large a value to increase the transport speed, the rail 
   616→will hit against the escaper and screws may fall into the machine from the clearance, failing 
   617→to unload screws normally. Adjust the vibration to a proper value that matches the loaded 
   618→screws. 
   619→For complementary information, see section Checking and adjusting the front/rear sides of the 
   620→rail page 19. 
   621→ 
   622→NOTE: 
   623→After making an adjustment, be sure to tighten the vibration plate fixing 
   624→bolt. 
   625→After making an adjustment, do an operational check.

   701→5.8 Checking and adjusting the timer  
   702→The screw transport feed differs depending on screw type. 
   703→This machine can make screw unloading smooth through timer adjustment. For screws with a 
   704→low transport speed, set the timer long. For screws with a high transport speed, set the timer 
   705→short. 
   706→This machine continues its operation when no screw is found at the screw pick up site. The 
   707→machine continues operating with a screw at the pickup site but will stop, after a certain lapse 
   708→of time, if the screw is not picked up. This time lapse can be varied by adjusting the timer. After 
   709→the screw is picked up, the machine starts operating again. 
   710→Check the operation by intercepting the optical axis of the sensor. 
   711→Make an adjustment with the timer knob at the rear of the machine body (as shown in the figure 
   712→on the right). 
   713→When the timer knob is turned clockwise, as viewed from the rear side, the time becomes 
   714→shorter. When the knob is turned counterclockwise, the time becomes longer. 
   715→Make this adjustment, by using the accompanying screwdriver, within the allowable 
   716→turning range, without using excessive force. 
   717→Do an operational check with screws loaded in the scooping chamber and set the timer 
   718→properly.

  1171→7.6.4 Explanation of the escaper movement before adjusting the sensor 
  1172→When the power switch is turned ON, if there is no screw at the site where the screw is to be 
  1173→picked up, the escaper rotates with the screw sensor LED off. 
  1174→The escaper rotates and accepts a screw from the rail. 
  1175→As the escaper rotates, it brings a screw to the screw pick up site. At this time, the sensor 
  1176→detects a screw, the LED lights up and the escaper stops. 
  1177→When the screw is removed from the pickup site, the LED light goes off and the escaper 
  1178→rotates to accept another screw from the rail. 
  1179→This is the correct sequence of operation. Usually, there is no need to adjust the sensor as it 
  1180→was done when assembled in the factory. 
  1181→The following are irregular situations that require adjustment: 
  1182→• There is no screw at the pickup site but, the LED is on and the escaper doesn't rotate 
  1183→• There's a screw at the pickup site, but the LED is not lit and the escaper rotates

  1376→10 Miscellaneous 
  1377→10.1 Overload Protective Circuit 
  1378→This machine is provided with an overload protective circuit. Usually, the driving motor 
  1379→rotates forward (normal rotation) to feed loaded screws to the escaper continuously, thus 
  1380→picking up screws one after another. However, if an overload is applied to the driving section, 
  1381→the driving motor rotates backward for a certain time and then returns to the normal 
  1382→rotation. When the cause of the overload is removed at the reverse rotation, the driving 
  1383→motor returns to the ordinary normal rotation and can pick up screws. 
  1384→If the cause of the overload is not removed during the reverse rotation, the driving motor 
  1385→repeats the sequence of reverse rotation - normal rotation - reverse rotation - normal rotation 
  1386→for a certain time to shut off the power to the driving motor. At this time, the escaper 
  1387→operation is not stopped. When the power to the driving motor is shut off, turn off the power 
  1388→switch and remove the cause of the overload. For example, when too many screws are put 
  1389→into the scooping chamber, reduce the quantity of loaded screws to a proper level. If any 
  1390→screw is caught by the driving section, remove it. 
  1391→After removing the cause of the overload, turn on the power switch to operate this machine.
