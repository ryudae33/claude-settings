  1022ŌåÆThe torque gradient shows how the torque is applied in the last phase of the Tightening

  1036ŌåÆThe friction between the screw thread and the hole thread is too low or too high
  1037ŌåÆŌĆó

  1346ŌåÆ8. Maintenance
  1347ŌåÆWARNING:
  1348ŌåÆAn overall inspection of OnRobot's end-of-arm tooling and accessories must be
  1349ŌåÆperformed regularly and at least once every 6 months. This inspection must
  1350ŌåÆinclude but is not limited to check for defective material and clean gripping
  1351ŌåÆsurfaces.
  1352ŌåÆUse original spare parts, and original service instructions for OnRobot's end-of-
  1353ŌåÆarm tooling, accessories and the robot. Failure to comply with this precaution
  1354ŌåÆcan cause unexpected risks, resulting in severe injury.
  1355ŌåÆIf you have questions regarding spare parts and repair, please visit our website
  1356ŌåÆwww.onrobot.com to contact us.
  1357ŌåÆ8.1. Screwdriver
  1358ŌåÆScrew Fix and Bit
  1359ŌåÆNOTE:
  1360ŌåÆThe screw fixes and bits are wear and tear parts, therefore they must be
  1361ŌåÆinspected regularly.
  1362ŌåÆThese parts must be inspected minimum once a week or after 60.000 cycles.
  1363ŌåÆIf a screw fix is damaged due to misuse, it must be replaced.
  1364ŌåÆIf the Pick Screw process is failing, it might be an indicator that the screw fix is worn or has
  1365ŌåÆbeen damaged.
  1366ŌåÆWARNING:
  1367ŌåÆThe Screw-bit System should be visually inspected every time the robot is
  1368ŌåÆreset or once per week, depending on application and use.
  1369ŌåÆWARNING:
  1370ŌåÆCheck the inside of the screw bit holder regularly for debris. Make sure that the
  1371ŌåÆmagnetic interface is clear from metal shavings and dirt.
  1372ŌåÆWARNING:
  1373ŌåÆAlways detach the Screwdriver from the Quick Changer to perform cleaning or
  1374ŌåÆmaintenance.

  1397ŌåÆpushing it down with the tool until it is out

  1400ŌåÆtool.
  1401ŌåÆ6. Insert the placement tool at the end of the

  1833ŌåÆ9. Troubleshooting
  1834ŌåÆ9.1. Robot Has Not Obtained an IP Address
  1835ŌåÆIf the Compute Box has not assigned an IP address to the robot, do the following:
  1836ŌåÆAssign a static IP address to the robot that matches your current IP settings on your Compute
  1837ŌåÆBox. The default IP address of the Compute Box is 192.168.1.1.
  1838ŌåÆNOTE:
  1839ŌåÆChange the last number in the IP address (if using 255.255.255.0 subnet
  1840ŌåÆmask) to avoid an IP conflict with the Compute Box.

  1842ŌåÆIf the default fixed (192.168.1.1) IP address is used on the Compute Box, then use the following
  1843ŌåÆvalues:

  1845ŌåÆIP address: 192.168.1.2
  1846ŌåÆŌĆó
  1847ŌåÆSubnet Mask: 255.255.255.0

  1849ŌåÆTo change the DIP switch network settings, first change the DIP switches and then cycle the
  1850ŌåÆCompute Box/Eye Box power so the changes will take effect.
  1851ŌåÆIf the changes still do not take effect, wait one minute and then cycle the Compute Box/Eye
  1852ŌåÆBox power once again.
